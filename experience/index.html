Programming Experience

I am a staff member of the Swarthmore College Computer Society (SCCS). We offer a variety of free web-based services to the Swarthmore College community, including web hosting, mailing lists, a campus directory, and a message board for campus news, jobs, events, etc. (the Reserved Students Digest). I work with SCCS on all parts of the stack, using primarily Python and Django.

I spent the summer of 2015 as a guest student at the Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution, where I worked in the lab of Dr. Scott Doney. My work consisted primarily of using MATLAB and the Python pandas data analysis library to analyze a 200,000-datapoint collection of measurements of radioactive tritium concentrations in precipitation, in order to determine the environmental effects of the nuclear weapons tests in the mid-20th century, which released massive amounts of tritium into the atmosphere.

Teaching Experience

I spent the summer of 2016 teaching Python and web development to 20 minority high school boys at All Star Code in New York City, hosted by Goldman Sachs. The curriculum assumed no prior programming knowledge, and started with introductory Python before moving on to web development using HTML, CSS, and Javascript, culminating in final projects which the students presented to parents, All Star Code donors, and Goldman Sachs employees at the end of the summer.

I spent the 2015-16 school year working part-time as a tutor for the Swarthmore Computer Science Department, helping first-year students prepare for assignments and examinations for the Python-based introductory computer science course.
